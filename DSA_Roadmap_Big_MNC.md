# DSA Roadmap for Big MNC Companies
## From Zero to Interview Ready

---

## 📋 Overview
This roadmap is designed for landing interviews at major tech companies (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple, Meta, etc.). It covers fundamental concepts through advanced problem-solving in **4-6 months** with consistent effort.

---

## Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-4)
**Goal:** Master basic programming and algorithmic thinking

### 1.1 Programming Basics Review
- **Language Selection:** Choose one (Java, Python, or C++)
  - Python: Easiest syntax, great for learning (recommended for beginners)
  - Java: Interview standard, good for scalability practice
  - C++: Fast execution, used in competitive programming
  
- **Topics to Cover:**
  - Variables, data types, operators
  - Control flow (if-else, loops)
  - Functions and scope
  - Arrays and basic string operations
  - Input/output handling

**Resources:**
- HackerRank's "Problem Solving" difficulty (Easy)
- LeetCode "Programming Basics" category
- Practice time: 3-4 hours/week

### 1.2 Big O Analysis (Critical Foundation)
- **Must Understand:**
  - Time complexity (O(1), O(log n), O(n), O(n log n), O(n²), O(2ⁿ))
  - Space complexity
  - Best/Average/Worst case scenarios
  - Why it matters in interviews

- **Practice:**
  - Analyze 5-10 simple algorithms
  - Practice: 2 hours/week

**Resources:**
- "Big O Notation" on YouTube (Abdul Bari)
- Introduction to Algorithms by CLRS (Chapter 3)

### 1.3 Problem-Solving Mindset
- **Framework:**
  1. Understand the problem completely
  2. Identify constraints (input size, time/space limits)
  3. Think of brute force first
  4. Optimize step by step
  5. Code cleanly
  6. Test with examples

**Practice:** Solve 30-40 "Easy" problems on LeetCode
**Time Investment:** 5-6 hours/week

## Phase 5: Mock Interviews & Final Polish (Weeks 25-26)
**Goal:** Test performance under interview conditions

### 5.1 Mock Interview Preparation
- **Platform:** LeetCode Premium, InterviewBit, Pramp, CodeSignal
- **Strategy:**
  - Take timed tests (45 minutes per problem)
  - Explain solution verbally
  - Discuss trade-offs
  - Write clean code

### 5.2 Interview Day Checklist
- Time: 60-90 minutes total
- 5 min: Clarifying requirements
- 10-15 min: Discuss approach before coding
- 25-35 min: Code the solution
- 5-10 min: Test and optimize
- 5 min: Discuss complexity and trade-offs

### 5.3 Practice Tests
- **Week 25:** 2-3 mock interviews
- **Week 26:** 2-3 mock interviews with focus on weak areas

## Study Schedule Template

### Weekly Breakdown (Sample Week)
- **Monday & Wednesday:** 2 hours - Learn new concept + 5 problems
- **Tuesday & Thursday:** 1.5 hours each - Practice 10 problems
- **Friday:** 2 hours - Review + discuss approach
- **Saturday & Sunday:** 2 hours each - Medium/hard problems on weaker topics

**Total:** ~15 hours/week for 6 months

### Daily Routine (2-3 hours)
1. **Warm-up (15 min):** Easy problem or review
2. **Learning (30-45 min):** New concept or difficult problem
3. **Practice (45-60 min):** 2-3 problems at current level
4. **Review (15 min):** Understand optimal solution if stuck

❌ Solving random problems without understanding patterns  
❌ Moving to hard problems too early  
❌ Not analyzing time/space complexity  
❌ Skipping problem explanation practice  
❌ Not reviewing solutions after solving  
❌ Memorizing instead of understanding  
❌ Neglecting edge cases  
❌ Writing messy code  
❌ Not practicing under time constraints  
❌ Ignoring weak areas

## Final Reminders

1. **Consistency beats intensity** - 2-3 hours daily is better than 10 hours once a week
2. **Understand before memorizing** - Know WHY, not just HOW
3. **Practice explaining** - Communication is 40% of the interview
4. **Quality over quantity** - Truly understand 200 problems vs. skimming 500
5. **Focus on patterns** - Problems repeat; patterns don't
6. **Stay confident** - You will struggle; that's normal and expected
7. **Rest matters** - Sleep, exercise, and breaks improve retention
8. **Track progress** - Monitor weak areas and allocate more time there
